Examples of the proof discovery process

We are trying to formulate a notion of "motivated proof". Roughly speaking, this means a proof that does not involve rabbits being drawn out of hats. While we do not yet have a precise notion yet, we do have several examples.

Two YouTube series of live problem-solving videos have been created by Tim Gowers. One focuses on problems that require the solver to find useful invariants, while the other is for problems that require clever colourings. We would be delighted to link to similar videos made by others (of any kind of non-routine problem -- they don't have to be IMO-style problems): please let us know of any that you know of, or have made yourself.

A list of challenge problems

The problems that we are trying to solve is a little different in style from the benchmark problems one finds in typical databases, in that they are more like the easier problems that one might find in a mathematics undergraduate problem set. So we would like to create our own collection of problems and use it to measure our progress. We have made a start on this and would welcome further suggestions.
